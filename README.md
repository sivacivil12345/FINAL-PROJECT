# 🚍 Transportation Revenue and Expenditure analysis

## 📌 Project Overview
This project presents an end-to-end exploratory data analysis (EDA) and business intelligence dashboard for Transportation funding analysis Dataset. The primary objective of this dataset is to analyze and compare transportation-related public finance statistics across U.S. states over multiple years and derive actionable insights to support data-driven transportation decision-making.

The project combines Python (Google Colab) for data preprocessing and exploratory analysis with Power BI for developing an interactive executive dashboard.

## 🎯 Project Objectives
◆ Clean and preprocess the raw transportation funding dataset.

◆ Perform Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) to identify patterns, trends, and outliers.

◆ Analyze transportation statics by :

    ◇ State
		
    ◇ Mode of transportation
		
    ◇ Year
		
◆ Study statistical properties such as skewness, kurtosis, and correlations.

## 📊 Key Insights

### 1. Overall Financial Performance

◆ The total transportation financial value is 285.91K crore.

◆ The combined population represented in the dataset is 4.04K crore.

◆ The analysis covers 52 states/territories, providing nationwide coverage.

◆ The average transportation value per population is 56.21, indicating the average financial allocation relative to population.

### 2. Revenue vs Expenditure

◆ Revenue contributes 76.38% (218.38K crore) of the total transportation finance.

◆ Expenditure accounts for 23.62% (67.53K crore).

◆ This indicates that transportation agencies generated substantially more revenue than they spent during the analysis period.

### 3. Transportation Mode Analysis

◆ Highways generate the highest transportation revenue among all transport modes.

◆ Transit has the highest population coverage, indicating that it serves the largest number of people.

◆ Air transportation contributes the smallest share of both revenue and population.

### 4. State-wise Performance

◆ Arizona records one of the highest transportation revenues.

◆ Tennessee and Georgia also demonstrate strong transportation financial performance.

◆ Wyoming shows comparatively higher expenditure than many other states.

◆ Financial performance differs significantly across states, reflecting variations in infrastructure investment and revenue sources.

### 5. Population vs Financial Value

◆ The scatter plot indicates a positive relationship between population and transportation financial value.

◆ States with larger populations generally generate higher transportation revenues.

◆ However, some states achieve relatively high financial values despite smaller populations, suggesting efficient revenue generation.

### 6. Year-wise Trend

◆ The average transportation financial value remained relatively stable over the three-year period:

     ◇ 2020: 31.02
     ◇ 2021: 30.93 (slight decline)
     ◇ 2022: 31.31 (highest average)
	 
◆ The increase in 2022 suggests a recovery in transportation funding and financial activity.

## 🚀 Recommendation

Transportation revenue is considerably higher than expenditure, with significant differences across states. Future planning should emphasize efficient revenue generation, optimized spending, equitable funding based on population, and inflation-adjusted financial analysis. Benchmarking high-performing states and improving data quality will enable more informed budgeting and better transportation policy decisions.

## ✅ Conclusion

This project demonstrates how Python-based data analytics combined with Power BI visualization can transform raw healthcare data into meaningful insights. The analysis shows that transportation finance varies considerably across states. Revenue transactions significantly outnumber expenditure transactions, indicating a stronger emphasis on revenue reporting. Larger states tend to generate higher transportation funds, while per-capita metrics reveal differences in spending efficiency. Inflation-adjusted values provide reliable year-to-year comparisons, making the dataset suitable for financial planning, policy formulation, and performance evaluation. Overall, the dataset offers valuable insights into transportation funding patterns and can support evidence-based public finance decisions.
